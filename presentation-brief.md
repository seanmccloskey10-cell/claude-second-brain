## The Problem

Every time you start a new Claude session, it has no idea who you are. You re-explain your business, your clients, your goals, your problems. Last week's insight? Gone. That article you discussed? Forgotten. You're starting from zero every single time.

You wouldn't hire an employee who forgets everything every Monday morning. Why accept that from your AI?

## The Fix

Your Second Brain is a folder of plain text files on your computer that Claude reads at the start of every session. The more you add, the smarter it gets.

**Two selling points, that's all:**
1. **New session, full context.** You say hello, Claude already knows your business. No repeating yourself. Ever.
2. **It gets smarter every week.** The more you put in, the more connections it finds. After a few weeks, it starts spotting patterns you wouldn't see alone.

## Just Talk to Claude

There are no commands to memorize. You just talk:

- **"I had an idea about pricing."** → Claude captures it in the right place.
- **"I saved an article to raw/. Can you process it?"** → Claude extracts what matters to your business.
- **"I want to think through my expansion plan."** → Claude interviews you, one question at a time.
- **"What patterns do you see across my vault?"** → Claude finds connections you missed.

Claude always shows you what it's going to write before making any changes. You approve everything.

One command worth knowing: **`/brief`** — run it once a week. Claude reads your entire vault and gives you a briefing: what's changed, what connects, and one thing to focus on.

## Three Habits

| Habit | What you do | Time |
|-------|------------|------|
| **Capture** | Tell Claude what's on your mind — voice-transcribe and go | 30 seconds |
| **Feed** | Save an article to `raw/`, tell Claude to process it | 2 minutes |
| **Review** | Run `/brief` once a week | 5 minutes to read |

That's the whole system. Claude handles the rest.

## Your Vault — What's Inside

```
your-vault/
├── pillars/       ← Your business (the core)
├── raw/           ← Articles, PDFs, posts (originals, never edited)
├── wiki/          ← Your personal Wikipedia (Claude builds this)
├── inbox/         ← Quick thoughts, not yet sorted
├── decisions/     ← Why you chose what you chose
└── mistakes-made  ← Your AI's error log — it never repeats them
```

**Two layers:**
- **You** capture thoughts, save articles, make decisions
- **Claude** organizes, connects, synthesizes, and remembers

## Features That Set This Apart

**The brain follows you everywhere.** Set up one file (2 minutes) and Claude reads your vault in every project you open — even unrelated ones. Open an empty folder, ask "what do you know about my business?" and Claude describes it from memory.

**Your AI learns from its mistakes.** There's a dedicated error log. When Claude gets something wrong, log it. Every future session reads that file first. It never makes the same mistake twice.

**Find connections you can't see.** Ask Claude to read your whole vault and spot patterns across your thinking. Or just run `/brief` weekly — it does this automatically. This is where the vault starts giving back more than you put in.

**Voice-first.** Talk instead of type — you'll say more and it takes less time. Claude cleans up the transcription automatically.

## Two Windows, One Brain

- **VS Code** — the workshop. Where you talk to Claude, capture thoughts, run `/brief`.
- **Obsidian** — the reading room. Where you browse your notes, follow links between ideas, see the big picture.

Same folder, two apps. VS Code builds the brain. Obsidian lets you explore it.

## The Compounding Effect

**Week 1:** A few notes. Feels sparse. That's normal.
**Month 1:** 10-20 pages. Claude starts connecting ideas you didn't connect yourself.
**Month 3:** You ask a question and Claude pulls from months of your own thinking plus every article you saved.
**Month 6+:** The vault is genuinely an advisor. It knows your history, your patterns, your blind spots. It pushes back when you're about to repeat a mistake.

Every thought compounds. Every article compounds. Every week compounds.
