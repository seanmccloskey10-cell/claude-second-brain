# Things You Can Ask Your Second Brain

Your vault doesn't have menus or buttons. You just talk to Claude. But if you're wondering "what can I actually do with this?" — here's a list of things you can ask, grouped by what you're trying to accomplish.

You don't need to memorize these. They're just ideas. Say them in your own words — Claude will figure out what you mean.

---

## Capture a thought

> "I had an idea about how to handle late cancellations — can you save it?"

> "Quick thought: I should follow up with my top 3 clients this week."

> "I keep telling people the same thing about why I started my business. Can you capture that so I don't lose it?"

Claude saves your thought, shows you where it would go, and writes it once you approve.

---

## Process an article or source

> "I saved an article to raw/. Can you read it and pull out what's useful for my business?"

> "There's a PDF in raw/ from a conference I went to. Can you turn it into notes?"

> "I copied a Twitter thread into raw/. What's in there that connects to what I'm already working on?"

Claude reads the source, extracts what's relevant to YOUR business (ignoring the rest), and creates wiki pages you can build on.

---

## Talk things through (brain dump)

> "I want to think out loud about my pricing. Can you interview me?"

> "A lot has changed in my business this month. Can you ask me about it and update my vault?"

> "I'm stuck on a decision. Can we talk it through?"

Claude asks you one question at a time, lets you talk, and then organizes everything into your vault. Great with voice-to-text.

---

## Find connections across your vault

> "Read my whole vault. What patterns do you see across my notes?"

> "Are there any ideas in my wiki that contradict each other?"

> "What connections am I not seeing between my recent articles and my business goals?"

> "Look at everything I've saved this month. What theme keeps coming up?"

This is one of the most powerful things the vault does. As your wiki grows, Claude can spot patterns across months of your thinking and reading that you'd never notice on your own. The weekly `/brief` does this automatically, but you can ask for it anytime.

---

## Get advice informed by your vault

> "Based on everything in my vault, how should I approach raising my prices?"

> "What have I said in the past about client retention? Remind me."

> "I'm writing a proposal. What do I know about this topic from my past research?"

Claude answers using your own past thinking and the articles you've saved — not generic advice, but advice grounded in YOUR context.

---

## Log a decision

> "I decided to stop offering group sessions and focus on 1:1. Can you log that?"

> "We're switching from hourly to package pricing starting next month."

Claude creates a decision file with the context, your reasoning, and a note about when to revisit it.

---

## Check vault health

> "Are there any notes that aren't linked to anything?"

> "Is there anything in raw/ that I haven't processed yet?"

> "Does my pillar file still match reality, or has my business changed?"

> "Is my instruction file up to date?"

Good to ask every month or so. Keeps the vault clean and current.

---

## Why this matters

The vault isn't a filing cabinet — it's an advisor that gets smarter the more you use it. Every thought, article, and decision adds context. Over time, Claude's answers stop being generic and start being specific to you, your business, and your situation.

The habit is simple: **capture daily, review weekly, and ask questions whenever you need them.** The vault does the rest.
